01
What this is

An online operation for operators.

The watching posture is over. The opening is wide for small teams of grounded operators wielding AI as leverage and working with the excellent. Decide / Act is the National Law Institute's first operating cadence: a vouched online convening that converts orientation into commitment.

The format is simple. Every operator in the cohort submits a five-field Brief by June 19. We synthesize the Briefs into one Operations Picture and distribute it to the room. We convene online for three working days, July 1–3, to calibrate, pair into pods, and commit. One week later we publish the Roster: who is doing what, where they are exposed, what matches exist, and the 90-day commitment each has put on the record. Ninety days later, we Pulse. Re-up against commitment, or be marked as didn't.

The orienting vocabulary is Brandon's The New Emergence of Operational Reality. The relevant distinction is structural, not ideological: grounded sovereign agents who bear the consequences of their decisions, versus meta-agents who operate without exposure to them. This convening is for the first kind.

+ This is for you if

You are inside a real fight.

  • You run a project, firm, household, campaign, or team where consequences land on you.
  • You can complete the five-field Brief without choking on any of it.
  • You want to leave with a named pod, named matches, and a 90-day commitment — not with notes.
  • You are willing to be vouched for, and to vouch.
− Probably not for you if

Your principal output is commentary.

  • You are looking for a stage, an audience, or a network to speak at.
  • You want ideological alignment as a precondition for collaboration.
  • You can't yet name a 90-day commitment that someone else could falsify.
  • You want to attend in order to be seen attending.

How it works

The operation.

Four phases over six weeks, plus a 90-day Pulse. Geographic dispersion is the feature. Every operator stays inside their own field of consequence while convening with the others.

I.
June 1 – June 19Pre-work window
The Brief

Pre-work is the price of admission.

Each invited operator submits a five-field Brief. No participation without it. NLI runs AI-assisted synthesis across all the Briefs and distributes a single Operations Picture to the cohort the weekend before the live week. Everyone arrives having already read everyone else.

01

How I read the terrain right now. Short, declarative.

02

Exposure

What I am actually on the hook for. The consequences I cannot exit.

03

Lane

The specific fight or build I am running inside that exposure.

04

Offer

What I can give to others — capability, access, leverage.

05

Ask

Where I need support. Named, concrete, actionable in 90 days.

II.
July 1 – July 3Live week · 3 days
The Sessions

Three days online. Working, not watching.

Three structured days held in a closed video room. No keynotes, no panels. Each day moves the cohort one step further into commitment, with a persistent async layer running between sessions for matching and side-conversations.

Day 01 — Calibrate Wed Jul 1

The Operations Picture, said together.

The Picture read into the room. Frame applied live. Opening exchanges between operators whose lanes already touch.

Day 02 — Pair Thu Jul 2

Pods, matched on offer / ask.

Working groups of four to six, surfaced from the Briefs. Real working sessions, with NLI staff as conduit. Cross-pod plenary at the close.

Day 03 — Commit Fri Jul 3

Public 90-day commitments.

Each operator names what they will do, with whom, by when. Pods commit to ongoing operation. The commitments enter the Roster.

III.
July 10T + 1 week
The Roster

One artifact, distributed to every operator.

One week after the Sessions, NLI publishes the Roster: a single internal document modeled on the situational assessment NLI produced from staff contributions in a previous cadence. Not a report about the convening. The convening, rendered as instrument: who is doing what, where they are exposed, the matches between them, and the commitments on the record.

The Pulse

Re-up. Or be marked as didn't.

Ninety days after commitment, an async pulse. Each operator updates their entry against the commitment they made. The Roster is republished, the field is visible to itself again, and the cadence continues quarterly thereafter. The heartbeat that keeps the network operating between conferences.

Six lanes

What gets worked on.

Topics are not panels. They are the lanes around which Briefs cluster and pods form. Each is a place where a 90-day commitment can credibly land.

/ 01 · Lane

Local pressure.

Showing up, weighting civic pressure, replacing representatives who won't move. Authority reattached to the county.

/ 02 · Lane

AI as outmaneuver.

Vibe coding, agent infrastructure, AI as leverage for small teams of the excellent. Not the discourse — the deployments. Compressing what used to require meta-agent scale.

/ 03 · Lane

Lane discipline.

Walking the decision-fitness check across current projects. Where are you acting outside your lane? Where are you being governed by people with exit privilege?

/ 04 · Lane

Household & subsidiarity.

The household as the first consequence-bearing unit, and therefore the first legitimate locus of decision. Continuity, integrity, succession.

/ 05 · Lane

Coalition without agreement.

Working with allies who don't share your read of events. A structural alliance of grounded agents beats an ideological alliance of meta-agents.

/ 06 · Lane

Signal hygiene.

The talking sphere as a domain of fifth-generation warfare. How to read it, use it, and not be used by it.

Common questions

FAQ

How do I get invited?

By vouch. Two paths: someone already in the network nominates you and underwrites your participation; or you request introduction below, naming the operators you know in our orbit. There is no public application.

If you don't yet know anyone, that is itself a real signal. Subscribe to the Pulse to be in range of future cohorts.

What is the time commitment?

Three commitments: the Brief (~90 minutes of focused writing in June), the live week (three days, July 1–3, ~16 hours total), and the Pulse (~30 minutes async in October). The 90-day commitment you make at the end is not a commitment to NLI — it is a commitment to your own field, with the network as witness.

What does it cost?

No fee for the 2026 cohort. The cost is the Brief and the 90-day commitment you carry out of it. Once you are vouched in, showing up honestly is the whole price.

What if I submit a Brief but can't attend the live week?

The live week is the spine. If you know in advance that you cannot be present for the bulk of July 1–3, defer to the next cohort — your Brief will carry. If an emergency pulls you out mid-week, your pod continues without you and you re-enter at the Pulse with an honest update against commitment.

Accessibility?

Live sessions run in a closed video room with live captions. Operations Picture, Roster, and Brief tooling are keyboard-navigable and screen-reader-tested. If you need a specific accommodation, name it in your Brief or email convening@statecraft.systems.

What time zones is this in?

Live sessions are anchored to Pacific Time (09:00 – 17:00 PT). Pods self-organize across time zones for their working sessions, with NLI staff facilitating cross-zone matching. The async layer runs continuously throughout the live week.

Will sessions be recorded?

Plenary portions only, for participants only. Pod sessions are not recorded. The Roster is the working record, not the video.

What happens with my Brief and my Roster entry?

Your Brief is read by NLI staff, ingested by the synthesis tooling, and surfaced into the Operations Picture for the cohort. Your Roster entry is distributed to participants only, never published outside the network without your consent. You can revise your entry at the Pulse, or request removal at any time.

Can I bring someone with me?

Once confirmed, you may vouch for up to two further operators on the same standard. A vouch is not a referral. It is an underwriting of that operator's productive participation.

Will there be an in-person event?

Not centrally. The format is online by design — geographic dispersion is the feature, not the bug. Pods that discover proximity are encouraged to convene in person inside their localities, on terms they set, in service of the work.

I don't fully agree with the Frame. Should I still come?

If your fight is real and your contribution to a pod would be useful, yes. The Frame is the orienting vocabulary, not a creed. What matters is that you can name your exposure, lane, offer, and ask — and that you mean to act on them.
