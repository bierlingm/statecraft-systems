Decide / Act // The Roster
The artifact the convening produces

The Roster.

Not a report about the convening. The convening, rendered as instrument. Sixty-four operators, their exposures, their matches, and the ninety-day commitments each has put on the record.

A conference produces a feeling. A report produces pages. The Roster produces a working record: one internal document that a real operator can act on the week after they receive it.

It is published once, one week after the live sessions, and republished at each quarterly Pulse with updates, deletions, and new entries. It is distributed only to operators inside the cohort. It is not public, and is not positioned to become so. The entries below are not mock-ups for a pitch deck. They are the format we actually use.

/ ShapeEach entry.

Every entry is a compressed picture of one operator's position in the field. Eight rows. No narrative sections, no bios. The Roster is a working instrument, not an anthology.

/ PrivacyThe four promises.

Operators submit Briefs with real information because the handling is real. Four things are true about every Roster entry:

01

Internal only.

The Roster is distributed to the cohort. It is never published outside the network, shared with press, or mined by third parties. Not now, not at the quarterly republish.

02

Pseudonymity on request.

If your fight requires it, your Operator row can be a handle. Locality can be regional rather than civic. Exposure and Lane still have to be specific enough to match against.

03

You control your entry.

Revise at any Pulse. Request removal at any time. Pods can opt to keep the cross-references without the originating entry if a member rotates out.

04

No second list.

There is no "observers" or "supporters" tier, no marketing mailing list derived from the Roster, and no list of rejected vouches kept anywhere. The Roster is the record. The Pulse subscription is separate.

/ LineageWhat it is modeled on.

The format is modeled on a situational assessment NLI produced from staff contributions in a previous cadence — the same exercise, done once, that showed us the instrument is more useful than the narrative. Decide / Act is that exercise, run across a cohort.

/ UseWhat operators do with it.

The week after distribution is the Roster's highest-velocity moment. People open it, find their matches, and initiate. Pods that formed during the Sessions have their working channel. Cross-pod matches begin. By the Pulse, most entries carry evidence: a call taken, a project split, a capability moved, a fight won or lost, a commitment met or not.

The Roster that republishes at the Pulse is the first version with weight. The second, the third, the fourth — that is the network operating.

Earn your entry.

Submission is the Brief. The Brief is the price. Every accepted Brief becomes an entry.
