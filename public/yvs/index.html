What this is. A first-draft, five-page static website for Yakima Valley Supply & Labor LLC, built verbatim from Tyler's own copy. No invented content. No backend. No dependencies beyond a web browser.

What this isn't. A finished product. Photography, refined branding, a real intake backend, and domain setup on Namecheap are all next passes — not this pass.

Context

Tyler's email described a small supply business in Prosser, WA selling jobsite consumables — gloves, coveralls, tape, brushes — to farms and remediation crews across Central Washington. He asked for help standing up a website on Namecheap and attached five Word documents covering the pages he wanted: Home, Industries We Serve, Ordering Process, Industrial Customer Intake Form, and Contact.

The copy is already strong — specific to how crews actually burn through supplies on the job. The task was to give it a home: clean typography, a work-grade aesthetic, and structure a first-time visitor can navigate without friction.

How to Leave Feedback

A small feedback widget is injected on every page. You'll see a floating marker in the lower corner. Click anywhere on the page to pin a comment to that exact element — a sentence, a button, a section header — and type what you'd change.

  1. Open any page in the list above.
  2. Click the element you want to comment on. The widget captures which thing on the page you're pointing at.
  3. Leave a note — as blunt or rough as you want. Name optional.
  4. That's it. Comments land server-side; I'll review them, reply, and revise.

This is easier than tracking changes in a document — especially for visual things ("make this smaller," "move this up," "this word feels wrong"). For Tyler and anyone else you want to loop in, the link is the whole interface.
