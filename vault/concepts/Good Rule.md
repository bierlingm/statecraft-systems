# Good Rule

The ultimate object of value that [[Statecraft Systems]] serves. An incontrovertibly, infinitely valuable good relative to who gets to benefit from it.

## The demand

There is unlimited demand for good rule. There is never enough of it. Every person, organization, community, and civilization benefits from good rule and suffers from its absence. This makes the work of producing good rule — forming rulers and building the [[Structures of Persistence]] they govern through — infinitely valuable work.

## What good rule requires

1. **Capable people** — rulers in the original sense: people who see reality clearly, make sound judgments, act with moral certainty, and accept the obligations of authority. See [[Counter-Elite]], [[Formation]].
2. **Viable structures** — [[Structures of Persistence]] that maintain good rule through time, encode [[Reciprocity]], and resist degradation.
3. **Reciprocal relationships** — good rule is not unilateral. It exists within structures of mutual obligation ([[Feudal Templates]], [[The Web]]).

## Revealed capacity

Everybody reveals the limits of their capacity for rule by who they grant responsibility and influence over them, and in what ways. A people that tolerates bad rulers reveals its own limits. This means the work of formation ([[Formation]]) applies not only to the rulers but to the ruled — the capacity to demand and sustain good rule must also be built.

This is why the work is never just about producing leaders. It is about producing the conditions — structural, relational, cultural — in which good rule can emerge and persist.

## The via negativa is a via positiva

[[Natural Law]] defines what must not be done — the constraints of viable cooperation. But these constraints do not enforce themselves. They require [[Structures of Persistence]] — specific, actively maintained structures that make the via negativa operative in reality. This is Moritz's core contribution to the [[Natural Law Institute]] body of work: the recognition that even negative constraints require positive construction.
