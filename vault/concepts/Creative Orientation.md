# Creative Orientation

The fundamental operating mode that [[Statecraft Systems]] works from and teaches, as defined in [[Structural Dynamics]] by [[Robert Fritz]].

The creative orientation means starting from a clear vision of what you want to create, holding that alongside an honest assessment of current reality, and advancing through the [[Structural Tension]] between the two.

This is contrasted with the [[Reactive Orientation]], where action is driven by problems, fears, or pressures. The creative orientation does not ignore problems — it subordinates them to the creative process rather than letting them drive it.

## Key properties

- **Vision-first** — clarity about the desired outcome comes before analysis of obstacles
- **Structural** — it works because of the underlying structure, not because of willpower or motivation
- **Scale-independent** — applies to an individual life, a product, a company, an institution, a civilization
- **Non-oscillating** — when the structure is set up correctly, progress does not reverse when pressure eases

## In practice at Statecraft Systems

Every engagement begins with: *What do you actually want?* and *What does reality actually look like?* — not *What's your problem?*
