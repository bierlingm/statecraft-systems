# Reciprocity

The operative principle of viable cooperation, central to [[Natural Law]] and foundational to [[Statecraft Systems]].

Structures that enforce reciprocal obligation — where rights come with duties, privileges with responsibilities, and extraction is constrained — tend to be viable. Structures that permit irreciprocal extraction tend to collapse or produce parasitic equilibria.

## Dimensions

- **Material reciprocity** — flows of resources, labor, and value are balanced over time
- **Informational reciprocity** — transparency and honest dealing; no asymmetric information exploitation
- **Obligational reciprocity** — duties match rights; authority comes with responsibility
- **Temporal reciprocity** — what is given now is returned over time; trust is built through demonstrated follow-through

## In structures

Reciprocity is not just an ethical principle — it is a structural constraint. Structures that violate reciprocity may function temporarily through coercion, deception, or subsidy, but they accumulate structural debt that eventually causes failure. This is a prediction from [[Natural Law]], not a moral preference.

## In the context of Statecraft Systems

[[The Web]] is governed by reciprocity. The [[Feudal Templates]] that inform our thinking about viable structures are characterized by mutual obligation running in both directions. The question applied to any proposed structure is: *Is this reciprocal? Will it hold?*
