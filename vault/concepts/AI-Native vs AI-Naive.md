# AI-Native vs AI-Naive

A distinction central to [[Statecraft Systems]]' practical work.

**AI-native** means you can actually use AI tools to create outcomes — you understand the tools well enough to build on them, iterate with them, and integrate them into your workflow as manipulable infrastructure.

**AI-naive** means you are still consuming pre-packaged products that someone else controls. You may use AI tools, but you use them as a consumer, not a builder. The tools shape your workflow; you do not shape the tools.

## Why the distinction matters

The gap between AI-native and AI-naive is a sovereignty gap. The AI-native operator has agency over their tools and workflows. The AI-naive operator is subject to whatever the platform vendors decide to build, deprecate, or monetize.

This gap is widening rapidly. As AI tools restructure every workflow, role, contract, and form of cooperation, the people who can wield these tools creatively will build what comes next. Everyone else will be reorganized by forces they do not understand.
