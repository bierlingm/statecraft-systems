# The Statecraft Thesis

**WORKING DRAFT — thesis sentence under active refinement.**

The thesis must express:
- [[Good Rule]] as an infinitely valuable good with unlimited demand
- The necessity of [[Structures of Persistence]] to maintain good rule across time
- The via negativa ([[Natural Law]]) requires via positiva (active construction)
- The craft dimension — this must be *built*, not merely declared
- [[The Intergenerational Challenge]] — rebuilding what was lost

Candidate formulations:
- "Good rule requires structures of persistence. We build them."
- "There is unlimited demand for good rule — and good rule requires specific structures of persistence that must be actively built and maintained."
- "The craft of building structures that persist good rule is the work. Everything else serves it."

The thesis operates from the [[Creative Orientation]], not the [[Reactive Orientation]]. It is a declaration of what is being created, not a diagnosis of what is broken.

## Relationship to the name

The thesis is encoded in the name [[Statecraft Systems]]. The name is not a metaphor. It is a compressed statement of the thesis itself. "Statecraft" = the craft of good rule. "Systems" = structures of persistence.

## Source disciplines

The thesis emerges from the synthesis of [[Structural Dynamics]], [[Natural Law]], [[Currency Design]], [[Collective Intelligence]], and [[Formation]].

## The core intellectual contribution

The recognition that the via negativa (Natural Law's constraints on what must not be done) is itself a via positiva — it requires [[Structures of Persistence]] to be enacted and maintained. This is Moritz's specific contribution to the [[Natural Law Institute]] body of work.
