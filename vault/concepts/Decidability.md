# Decidability

A key concept from [[Natural Law]] as developed by [[Curt Doolittle]].

Decidability is the capacity to determine what is true, what is reciprocal, and what is viable in human cooperation. It is the epistemic foundation that makes [[Sovereignty]] possible — you cannot self-determine if you cannot determine what is real.

## What it provides

- A means of evaluating claims and proposals against reality
- The ability to distinguish viable structures from non-viable ones
- A framework for identifying irreciprocal extraction before it collapses a system
- The discipline of looking at structure, externalities, and tradeoffs rather than packaging

## Connection to the work

Decidability was one of the four disciplines taught in the original [[Modern Minuteman]] program (alongside Testimony, Cooperation, and Statecraft). It represents the capacity to judge — to evaluate information, proposals, and structures on their actual merits rather than on rhetoric, emotion, or social pressure.

In a world where [[Information Sovereignty]] is contested and manipulation is the dominant strategy, decidability is not academic. It is survival-grade capability.
