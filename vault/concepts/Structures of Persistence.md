# Structures of Persistence

The precise object of [[Statecraft Systems]]' work. The concept that bridges [[Natural Law]]'s theoretical framework and the practical craft of building.

## The core insight

Moritz's specific contribution to the [[Natural Law Institute]] body of work, which [[Curt Doolittle]] has agreed with:

Even the via negativa — the negative constraints, the definition of what must not be done — is itself a via positiva. It must be actively enacted. Knowing what is prohibited is insufficient. The prohibitions must be maintained through specific structures that persist across time, across challenges, across generations.

You cannot just define the rules of good rule. You must build the structures that *keep* good rule in place.

## What "persistence" means

A structure of persistence is one that:
- Maintains its function through time, not just at the moment of creation
- Survives the departure of any individual ([[Systems]])
- Resists degradation from internal and external pressures
- Actively reproduces the conditions for its own continuation
- Transmits its principles to the next generation ([[The Intergenerational Challenge]])

This is the distinction between a rule and an institution. Rules can be ignored. Structures of persistence make the rule the path of least resistance — see [[Structural Dynamics]].

## Why this is the core

[[Structural Dynamics]] provides the engine of creation. [[Natural Law]] provides the constraints. But the *what* — the thing actually being created — is structures of persistence. Structures that maintain good rule.

This is what "Statecraft Systems" ultimately means: the craft of building structures that persist good rule across time.

## Relationship to other concepts

- [[Reciprocity]] — persistent structures enforce reciprocity structurally, not just normatively
- [[Feudal Templates]] — historical examples of structures designed for persistence (mutual obligation codified in lasting institutional form)
- [[Formation]] — people must be formed to maintain the structures, and the structures must form the people who come after
- [[Oscillation]] — structures that do not persist produce oscillation; the system regresses to its baseline
- [[The Intergenerational Challenge]] — the ultimate test of persistence is whether it survives across generations
