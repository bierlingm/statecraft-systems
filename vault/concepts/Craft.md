# Craft

The human element in [[Statecraft Systems]]. Craft implies:

- **Direct contact with the material** — not delegated, not abstracted, not managed from a distance
- **Honest assessment of what is** — the craftsman cannot lie about the state of the material
- **Skilled making** — accumulated capability through practice, not theory alone

Craft is distinguished from administration, management, and engineering-in-the-sterile-sense. It carries the connotation of the hand on the work — the [[Frontiersman]], the [[Bell-Maker]], the builder who knows the material because they have worked it.

In the context of Statecraft Systems, craft applies to:
- Building AI tools on real workflows (technical craft)
- Designing structures of cooperation (structural craft)
- [[Formation]] of people through direct experience (formative craft)

The [[Monastery Meets Workshop]] aesthetic reflects this: the workshop is the space of craft.
