# Calibration Framework

The principled means of deciding, for any given piece of content, how much of the full worldview to surface. Replaces ad-hoc decisions about "how explicit should we be" with a structural approach.

## Levels

**Level 1 — Professional surface.** States what [[Statecraft Systems]] does, for whom, and how to engage. Clean, factual, direct. A competent executive should be able to read this and know whether to reach out.
*Applies to:* Home page (above the fold), Contact, service/offering descriptions.

**Level 2 — Structural depth.** Explains the methodology, the disciplines, the reasoning. Requires engagement from the reader. Filters for people who think structurally.
*Applies to:* Approach page, detailed service descriptions, tool descriptions.

**Level 3 — Full thesis.** The complete worldview: [[Good Rule]], [[Structures of Persistence]], the intergenerational challenge, [[Operative Traditions]]. Attracts allies and repels the uncommitted.
*Applies to:* Thesis page (if published), About page (selectively), deep content.

## Principle

Every page is internally consistent at its calibration level. Do not mix Level 1 and Level 3 language on the same page without a clear structural reason.

## Connection to [[Operative Aesthetics]]

The visual design mirrors the calibration framework. The surface is austere and functional (Level 1). Precision details reveal themselves to those who engage (Level 2). The full richness of the work rewards sustained attention (Level 3). The visitor earns depth.
