# Structural Tension

The core mechanism of [[Structural Dynamics]] as developed by [[Robert Fritz]].

Structural tension is created by holding two things simultaneously:
1. **The desired outcome** — what you actually want
2. **Current reality** — what actually exists right now

The discrepancy between these two creates a tension that seeks resolution. If the structure is set up correctly, this tension resolves in the direction of the desired outcome — the system advances.

This is not metaphorical. It is a description of how structures behave. A rubber band stretched between two points will seek to resolve the tension. If the "desired outcome" end is anchored, resolution moves current reality toward it. If both ends are free, the system oscillates.

## Why it matters

Structural tension is what distinguishes genuine creative advance from [[Oscillation]]. Without it, effort dissipates or reverses. With it, the path from current reality to desired outcome becomes structurally favored.

## In practice

Every engagement at [[Statecraft Systems]] begins by establishing structural tension: extreme clarity about what the client actually wants and honest assessment of where they actually are. This is often uncomfortable. Most people have not been asked to be this clear about either.
