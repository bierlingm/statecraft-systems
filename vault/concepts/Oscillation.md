# Oscillation

The structural pattern that occurs when effort produces partial results, then regresses, then renews — cycling without genuine advance. A key concept in [[Structural Dynamics]].

Oscillation is not caused by lack of effort, discipline, or intelligence. It is a structural property. When the underlying structure does not favor resolution toward the desired outcome, the system will oscillate regardless of how hard anyone works.

## Common forms

- A company that grows, then contracts, then grows again — never breaking through
- A person who starts a health regimen, succeeds for a while, then slides back
- An organization that solves a problem, only to find it return in a different form
- A venture that achieves traction, then loses momentum as the initial urgency fades

## The structural cause

In the [[Reactive Orientation]], the motivation to act comes from the problem. As the problem recedes, motivation recedes. The system returns to its baseline. This is why "working in the business" rather than "on the business" is a trap — it is structurally oscillatory.

## The resolution

[[Structural Tension]] set up correctly — with a clear desired outcome anchored in place — creates a structure that favors advance over oscillation. This is the core work of [[Structural Dynamics]].
