# Sovereignty

Self-determination by self-determined means. This is the technical definition from [[Natural Law]].

Sovereignty is not a claim or a right. It is a capacity — created and maintained through structures of relationship. No individual maintains sovereignty alone against sufficient opposition. This has always been true.

## Structural nature

Sovereignty requires:
- **Alliances** — mutual obligations with others who share interests
- **Capability** — the competence to act effectively
- **Information processing** — the capacity to evaluate, filter, and act on information correctly and quickly
- **Structures** — systems that hold these relationships and capabilities in place over time

In the absence of these, "sovereignty" is an aspiration, not a reality.

## In the current moment

The prevailing strategies favor cognitive and information manipulation over kinetic engagement — because it is generally cheaper. Psyops, misinformation, narrative control. This means sovereignty today requires information processing capacity in a contested environment at higher speeds than ever before (see [[OODA Loop]]).

This is where [[AI-Native vs AI-Naive]] capability and structural awareness converge most directly. The tools exist to process information at unprecedented scales and speeds. The question is whether you wield them in service of your own aims, or whether they wield you in service of someone else's.
