# Success That Succeeds

A phrase from [[Robert Fritz]] and [[Structural Dynamics]] that captures a critical distinction.

Not all success is structurally sound. Some successes create the conditions for their own reversal — they succeed in the moment but contain structural flaws that guarantee future failure. A startup that grows through unsustainable burn. A leader who achieves results through coercion that erodes loyalty. A product that wins market share through subsidies that cannot continue.

"Success that succeeds" means outcomes that are structurally sound — that do not create the conditions for their own reversal. The success is stable because the underlying structure supports it.

## Requires

- [[Structural Tension]] set up correctly
- [[Creative Orientation]] rather than [[Reactive Orientation]]
- Honest assessment of current reality (no self-deception about costs or tradeoffs)
- Structures that enforce [[Reciprocity]] rather than permit extraction

## In practice

At [[Statecraft Systems]], this is the standard. The question is never just "did it work?" but "will it keep working? Does the structure support ongoing advance, or does it contain the seeds of regression?"
