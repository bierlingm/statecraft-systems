# Curt Doolittle

Developer of the [[Natural Law]] framework. Associated with the [[Natural Law Institute]].

## Key contributions

- The operationalization of Natural Law as a testable, decidable framework
- [[Reciprocity]] as the operative principle of viable cooperation
- [[Decidability]] — frameworks for determining truth and viability
- [[Sovereignty]] defined as "self-determination by self-determined means"
- The study of what works and what does not, given the actual constraints of human nature

## Relationship to Statecraft Systems

One of the foundational influences. The original intent behind the name [[Statecraft Systems]] was to operationalize Doolittle's body of work. The first product ([[Modern Minuteman]]) taught four disciplines from Natural Law: Testimony, [[Decidability]], Cooperation, and Statecraft — with Moritz teaching Statecraft and three Fellows of the Institute teaching the others.

Natural Law provides the reality lens and viability constraints that complement [[Structural Dynamics]]' creative engine.

## Public association

Named publicly as an influence. His work is referenced explicitly in the context of Natural Law, reciprocity, and sovereignty.
