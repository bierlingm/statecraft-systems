# Frontiersman

One of the load-bearing metaphors for the experience of working with [[Statecraft Systems]].

## The image

A frontiersman who can tell you about the very real opportunities and dangers of the frontier between hunting parties, gold prospecting, horse taming, cattle herding, or cabin construction. Someone who has been in the territory, knows it firsthand, and can guide you through it.

## What it carries

- **Vitality** — the energy of someone who builds real things in open territory, absent from those inside well-guarded structures
- **Direct experience** — not theoretical knowledge, but knowledge earned through contact with the material
- **Competence under real conditions** — no bureaucratic safety net
- **Generosity** — the frontiersman shares knowledge of the territory because capable neighbors are better than helpless ones

## Where it applies

The [[Unlock Session]] has this character — sitting with someone who knows the frontier and showing them the lay of the land. The ongoing [[Sparring Partner]] relationship has it too — the frontier keeps moving, and you want someone who has been exploring it alongside you.

## Relationship to other metaphors

The frontiersman is the active/outward dimension. The [[Monastery Meets Workshop]] is the spatial/environmental dimension. The [[Bell-Maker]] is the heritage/craft dimension. Together they triangulate the Statecraft Systems experience.
