# Bell-Maker

A metaphor drawn from Moritz's family history. On his father's side: bell makers and leather makers, small industrialists. His grandfather became a director at Siemens.

## What the bell-maker represents

- **Creating something that pulls people together** — the bell as a coordinating instrument
- **Supporting collective coherence** — the sound that orients a community
- **Upward orientation** — church bells, civic bells, the call to something beyond the individual
- **Deep craft** — bell-making is skilled, precise, consequential work
- **Durability** — bells last for centuries; they are infrastructure, not consumables

## Connection to Statecraft Systems

The bell-maker is a metaphor for what [[Statecraft Systems]] creates: structures, tools, and systems that pull people together, support coherence, and orient upward. Like a bell, a well-designed system serves a community long after the maker's direct involvement ends.

## The broader family pattern

The progression from bell-makers → small industrialists → Siemens director maps to the Statecraft Systems pattern: from craft to infrastructure to a vast variety of practical products, services, and infrastructure. This is coherent with the work.

## The Medici parallel

Also referenced: the Medici as holders of rich patterns relevant to the work — patrons, builders, network-weavers who created the conditions for cultural and institutional flourishing through strategic investment in people and institutions.

## Usage

Potentially part of the brand story. The bell-maker heritage gives the work historical rootedness and a specific image that is distinctive, non-corporate, and carries the right connotations.
