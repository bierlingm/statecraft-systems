# Feudal Templates

The observation that feudal structures offer viable templates for reciprocal relationships — care, duty, obligation, rights, privileges running in both directions.

## The insight

Not ideological posturing about the aesthetic appeal of knights and lords. A structural observation: feudal structures, stripped of their romantic packaging, encode patterns of mutual obligation that are more sophisticated and more viable than many modern alternatives.

Specifically:
- **Rights come with responsibilities** — no privilege without duty
- **Loyalty runs in both directions** — lords have obligations to vassals, not just the reverse
- **Authority comes with care** — the strong have duties to the communities they lead
- **Relationships are long-term and reciprocal** — not transactional

## The point

"Don't look at the packaging, look at what's inside. Look at structure, externalities, tradeoffs. Become capable of judging things." The feudal template is not about recreating medieval society. It is about recognizing structural patterns that work and applying them to contemporary challenges.

## Calibration for the website

Not necessarily prominent. The substance (mutual obligation, duty, care, loyalty, reciprocal structure) belongs on the site. The label "feudal" may or may not — this is a calibration decision. Moritz is not afraid of the word but also does not need to make a thing of it.
