# Collective Intelligence

The capacity to organize and adapt continuously at group scale.

Draws on work in distributed cognition, stigmergic coordination, and adaptive systems. Related to [[Arthur Brock]]'s work and connected to [[Currency Design]].

## What it addresses

How groups can be smarter, faster, and more resilient than any individual member. Specifically:
- How groups make good decisions together
- How groups learn from collective experience
- How groups reorganize in response to changing conditions
- How this happens without requiring central authority to direct every move

## Role in the synthesis

Collective intelligence provides the *group dimension* — the means by which [[Structural Dynamics]] and [[Natural Law]] scale beyond the individual. It intersects with [[Currency Design]] (how flows enable group cognition) and [[Formation]] (how people are prepared to participate in collective intelligence).

## In practice

Relevant to:
- Team design and coordination at [[Statecraft Systems]]
- The structure of [[Modern Minuteman]] cohorts
- The design of [[The Web]] as a self-reinforcing network
- How AI tools can augment group information processing ([[Information Sovereignty]])
