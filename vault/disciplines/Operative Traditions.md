# Operative Traditions

The study and practice of traditions as living, enacted structures — not inherited customs but active operations that must be performed to persist. Associated with [[Miguel Angel Fernandez]].

## Core insight

A tradition is operative when it is practiced, not merely remembered. The distinction between an operative tradition and a dead one is whether it is actively enacted by people who understand its function and carry it forward through their conduct.

This parallels the core insight of [[Statecraft Systems]]: the via negativa is a via positiva. Constraints, principles, and values do not maintain themselves. They require [[Structures of Persistence]] — and traditions, properly understood, are among the oldest and most proven forms of such structures.

## Role in the synthesis

Operative Traditions contributes to the understanding of *how* structures persist across generations ([[The Intergenerational Challenge]]). Where [[Structural Dynamics]] provides the engine of creation, [[Natural Law]] provides the constraints, and [[Currency Design]] provides the architecture of flows, Operative Traditions provides the understanding of how knowledge, practice, and principle are transmitted through time via living enactment.

This connects directly to [[Formation]] — the embodied transmission of capability through direct experience and practice.

## Relationship to other disciplines

- [[Natural Law]] — provides the content (what must be maintained); Operative Traditions addresses the form (how it is maintained through practice)
- [[Formation]] — the process by which individuals are brought into operative traditions
- [[Structural Dynamics]] — traditions that are operative create [[Structural Tension]] toward their own continuation; dead traditions do not
- [[Feudal Templates]] — historical structures that persisted in part because they were operatively maintained through ritual, obligation, and practice
