# Currency Design

The architecture of flows. Drawn from the work of [[Arthur Brock]] and others working in [[Collective Intelligence]] and distributed systems.

## What "currency" means here

Not limited to money. A currency is any formal system that shapes how value and information move. Examples:
- Reputation systems
- Credentialing
- Mutual credit
- Governance tokens
- Attention markets
- Obligation tracking

All of these are currencies in the structural sense — they make flows legible, shapeable, and accountable.

## What it provides

Tools for structuring flows of information, value, obligation, and resources within and among groups. Designing these well means designing the incentives, feedback loops, and information flows that make cooperation legible and sustainable.

## Role in the synthesis

Currency design provides the *architecture of flows* within the structures that [[Structural Dynamics]] creates and [[Natural Law]] constrains. It answers: how does value move? How is obligation tracked? How do people see what is happening in the system?

This is directly relevant to organizational design, team coordination, and the broader institutional structures that [[Statecraft Systems]] helps build.
