# Natural Law

The lens for seeing reality clearly and the constraint set for building viable structures. As developed by [[Curt Doolittle]] and practiced at the [[Natural Law Institute]].

## What it provides

- A framework for [[Decidability]] — determining what is true, what is reciprocal, and what is viable
- The constraints of human nature, power dynamics, and resource competition made explicit
- The principle of [[Reciprocity]] as the operative test for structural viability
- Means of seeing reality without the distortions of ideology, wishful thinking, or social pressure

## Key concepts

- **Reciprocity** — the foundational principle of viable cooperation ([[Reciprocity]])
- **Decidability** — the capacity to determine truth and viability ([[Decidability]])
- **Sovereignty** — self-determination by self-determined means ([[Sovereignty]])
- **Testimony** — one of the four disciplines taught in [[Modern Minuteman]]

## The via negativa is a via positiva

Moritz's core contribution to the Natural Law body of work: even the via negativa — the constraints, the definition of what must not be done — is itself a via positiva. It must be actively enacted through [[Structures of Persistence]]. Knowing the constraints is necessary but not sufficient. The constraints must be made operative through specific, actively maintained structures. This is the bridge between Natural Law's theoretical framework and [[Statecraft Systems]]' practical work.

## Role in the synthesis

Natural Law provides the *what is viable* — the reality constraints that any proposed structure must satisfy. [[Structural Dynamics]] provides the engine of creation; Natural Law tells you what will actually hold once built. And the recognition that the via negativa requires positive construction tells you *what must be built*: [[Structures of Persistence]].

This is not ideology. It is the study of what works and what does not, given the actual nature of human beings. It informs assessment of whether a proposed structure, agreement, or strategy will survive contact with reality.

## Origin of Statecraft Systems

The original name choice was largely based on the intent to operationalize the body of work of Natural Law. The first product ([[Modern Minuteman]]) taught four disciplines from this body of work: Testimony, Decidability, Cooperation, and Statecraft.
