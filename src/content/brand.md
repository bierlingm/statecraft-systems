# Statecraft Systems — Brand

This document governs how the thesis is expressed. Every visual, verbal, and structural decision on the site and in public-facing materials must be traceable to it.

## Voice

**Register.** Direct, unhurried, precise. Sentences state facts or identify structural relationships. We do not persuade through emphasis or emotional appeal. We persuade through clarity.

**Person.** First person plural ("we") for Statecraft Systems as an entity. First person singular ("I") for Moritz when speaking personally. Third person when describing the work from outside.

**Vocabulary.**

Use freely: structure, craft, rule, capacity, reciprocity, obligation, sovereignty, formation, advance, operative, discipline, viable, persist, transmit, build.

Use with care: framework, ecosystem, mission, vision, journey. These are common enough to be invisible but vague enough to mean nothing. Use only when the specific meaning is clear from context.

Do not use: solutions, innovative, cutting-edge, leverage (as verb), passionate, excited, empower, holistic, synergy, disrupt, unlock (as metaphor), deep dive, game-changer. Also: no emojis, no exclamation marks, no rhetorical questions addressed to the reader.

**Cadence.** Short declarative sentences as the baseline. Longer sentences when the structure of an idea requires it, not for rhythm or variation. No sentence exists for its sound alone.

**Honesty standard.** Every claim on the site must be something Moritz can defend in a direct conversation. No positioning language that inflates scope, implies capabilities not yet demonstrated, or gestures at depth that is not there. If something is early-stage, say so. If something is aspirational, mark it as such.

## Visual Principles

### Design Philosophy

The design addresses the full human being, not a narrow economic or informational case. Good architecture, good carpentry, good typography all approach a point of organic wholeness where function and form mutually inform and support one another and grow into one. Gothic cathedrals do this. Art Nouveau does this. The best military and industrial print does this. The design of Statecraft Systems should do this.

The site is itself a structure of persistence. It should be built the way we build everything: with structural soundness, operative clarity, and precision in detail.

### Operative Aesthetics

The design is operative in the same way the traditions are operative. It works because every element has a function. Nothing decorative exists for its own sake. But functional elements, executed with care, produce beauty as a byproduct of structural soundness. A line that terminates in a precise geometric form. A subtle shift on interaction that rewards attention. A typographic hierarchy that teaches the reader how to navigate without being told.

The visitor earns depth. The surface is austere and functional. Precision details reveal themselves to those who look closely or engage further. This mirrors the calibration framework: Level 1 is clean utility; Level 2 shows the structural thinking; Level 3 rewards sustained attention with the full richness of the work.

Examples of this principle:
- A rule line that carries a small geometric terminal (fleur-de-lis, cross, or abstract form) visible on close inspection
- Hover states that reveal additional structure rather than merely changing color
- Typographic details that distinguish levels of content without being decorative
- Transitions between pages or sections that feel architectural (thresholds, not animations)

### Reference Points

These inform the feel without being imitated directly:

- **The New Swiss Passport** (RETINAA, retinaa.ch): the definitive reference. Clean yet complex. Every element serves both a security function and an expressive purpose simultaneously. Layers of information revealed under different conditions (UV light reveals topography, architectural landmarks, hidden structure). Cartographic precision as aesthetic. The entire document is a Gesamtkunstwerk of identity expressed through structural design. This is what "earned depth" looks like in physical form.
- **U.S. Graphics Company** (usgraphics.com): the makers of Berkeley Mono. Their design philosophy is directly aligned: "emergent over prescribed aesthetics," "dense not sparse," "performance is design," "as complex as it needs to be," "don't infantilize users." Regimented functionalism that produces beauty through precision, not ornamentation.
- **Triple Aught Design** (tripleaughtdesign.com): technical utility and craft coexisting. No wasted gesture. Every element serves a purpose but the total effect is considered and specific.
- **Army industrial design and print**: authority, utility, information hierarchy enforced by layout. Zero decoration for its own sake.
- **Pre-war Dresden architecture**: formal beauty that is also civic infrastructure. Seriousness expressed through proportion and material, not through austerity alone.
- **Kunststätte Bossard**: Gesamtkunstwerk, total artwork. Every element in service of a unified vision. The site as a coherent whole, not a collection of pages.
- **Gothic cathedrals, Art Nouveau, papal architecture**: organic wholeness where function and form are inseparable. Designed for the full human being. Structure that elevates.

### Typography

Berkeley Mono remains the primary typeface. Its monospaced precision communicates technical capability and care. Additional typefaces may be introduced to serve specific functions:
- A serif or humanist face for longer contemplative content (thesis, approach) if the monospace proves fatiguing at length
- The choice should emerge from the design system phase, tested against real content

### Color

The palette flows from the other design decisions. It is not predetermined. Starting constraints:
- Warmth over coolness (this is not a tech company)
- Sufficient contrast for long reading (the content is dense)
- The accent color must feel structural, not decorative: it marks thresholds, actions, and distinctions, not headings for the sake of visual relief

### What It Must Never Look Like

- Corporate: blue/grey, sans-serif, stock photography
- Startup-slick: gradients, animations, dark mode with neon accents
- Guru/personal-brand: large hero photo, "Book a Call" floating button, testimonial carousels
- Minimalist-for-minimalism's-sake: empty space without purpose
- Precious or fragile: the aesthetic must carry weight, not delicacy

## Metaphor System

The thesis and interview produced several metaphors. Their roles are distinct.

**Load-bearing (use across the site):**
- **Craft** — direct contact with the material; the hand on the work. Present in the name, in how services are described, in the visual feel.
- **Structure/structural** — the central vocabulary. Structures advance or oscillate. Structures persist or degrade. Everything is evaluated structurally.
- **Operative** — alive because enacted, not because declared. Applies to traditions, to the practice, to the web of relationships.

**Contextual (use where relevant, not everywhere):**
- **Frontier** — describes the current moment. Appropriate on pages addressing the present situation or the nature of the opportunity. Not a permanent identity marker.
- **Formation** — describes the human transmission dimension. Appropriate when discussing training, retreats, cohort programs, and the development of people.
- **Sovereignty** — describes the outcome of the work at the political/personal level. Appropriate on approach-oriented pages and in the thesis. Not a sales word.

**Background (inform tone and decisions, do not surface as language):**
- **Frontiersman** — informs the felt experience of working with Moritz. Does not appear as a word on the site.
- **Bell-maker** — informs the heritage story. May appear on the About page if it serves. Does not recur.
- **Feudal templates** — informs the structural thinking about reciprocal obligation. The substance (mutual obligation, duty running in both directions) appears. The word "feudal" does not need to.
- **Monastery meets workshop** — informs the visual and spatial feel. Does not appear as a phrase. The site should feel like this without naming it.

## Calibration Framework

The thesis is written at full conviction. Not everything on the site operates at that depth. Each page has a calibration level.

**Level 1 — Professional surface.** States what Statecraft Systems does, for whom, and how to engage. Clean, factual, direct. No philosophical framing required. A competent executive should be able to read this and know whether to reach out.
*Applies to:* Home page (above the fold), Contact, service/offering descriptions.

**Level 2 — Structural depth.** Explains the methodology, the disciplines, the reasoning behind the approach. Requires some engagement from the reader. Filters for people who think structurally.
*Applies to:* Approach page, detailed service descriptions, tool descriptions.

**Level 3 — Full thesis.** The complete worldview: good rule, structures of persistence, the intergenerational challenge, operative traditions. This level attracts allies and repels the uncommitted. It is the real thing.
*Applies to:* Thesis page (if published), About page (selectively), deep content.

Every page should be internally consistent at its calibration level. Do not mix Level 1 and Level 3 language on the same page without a clear structural reason.

## Brand Architecture

**Statecraft Systems** is the primary entity. Everything else relates to it.

**Productions (built by Statecraft Systems for others or for itself):**
- Modern Minuteman (modernminuteman.net) — own program
- The Field Company (fieldcompany.org) — own program
- Hayes Canon (hayescanon.org) — production for Brandon Hayes
- Capable Few (capablefew.com) — own project

These are presented as "productions" or "projects," not "sub-brands." They have their own identities but are credited to Statecraft Systems.

**Tools (built and used internally, shared with the network):**
- sd-core — structural dynamics framework (flagship)
- werk — the operative instrument (CLI + TUI, built on sd-core)
- spikes.sh — rapid prototyping

These are presented as tools, not products. They demonstrate capability and serve the network.

**Moritz Bierling** appears as the founder and practitioner. The site is about Statecraft Systems, not about Moritz personally, but his background, formation lineage, and direct involvement are part of the credibility. The About page carries his story. Service descriptions can reference his direct involvement. moritzbierling.com is linked but separate.

**The web** is described as a principle (reciprocity, mutual reinforcement) rather than listed as an org chart. It is felt through the connections between productions, tools, and people, not through a diagram.

## Naming Conventions

**Offerings** use plain descriptive names: Unlock Session, Personal Stack Build, Sparring Partner, Discovery, Build, Train, Retainer. No invented terminology. No proprietary-sounding names. The names say what the thing is.

**Disciplines** are named by their established names: Structural Dynamics, Natural Law, Currency Design, Collective Intelligence, Operative Traditions, Formation. We did not invent these (except the synthesis). Credit the originators.

**Concepts** from the thesis (structures of persistence, via negativa/positiva, structural tension, creative/reactive orientation) are used as working vocabulary, not branded terms. They appear in lowercase unless at the start of a sentence. They are explained when first used on any given page, then used freely.

## Differentiation

A visitor should be able to distinguish Statecraft Systems from other entities within seconds, through feel alone.

**From AI consultancies:** They sell implementation and strategy. We build capability directly, on the person's own devices, and the methodology extends far beyond technology. The visual system has no tech-company signals (no dark mode, no terminal-green, no "API-first" language).

**From corporate strategy firms:** They produce reports and recommendations. We produce structural change through direct work. The site has no stock photography, no team grid, no case-study carousel.

**From personal brand / guru sites:** They sell access to a personality. We are building an operative tradition that outlasts any individual. The site does not center Moritz's face, does not use testimonial sliders, does not have a "Book a Call" button as the primary CTA.

**From startup-aesthetic companies:** They signal speed, growth, and disruption. We signal persistence, craft, and structural soundness. The site is warm where they are cool, slow where they are fast, heavy where they are light.
